\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
This class describes a transformation of variables according to $ d^\mathrm{i}_{kl} = \dfrac{1}{2}\left( F_{ki} {U^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{ij} \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{jm} F^{-1}_{ml} + F_{li} {U^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{ij} \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{jm} F^{-1}_{mk} \right) $, where $\dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{kl}$ and ${U^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{kl}$ are symmetric, while $F_{kl}$ and $F^{-1}_{kl}$ are unsymmetric.

Given a function $f(d^\mathrm{i}_{kl})$, the result is a function $g(\dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{mn}, U^{-1}_{mn}, F_{mn}, F^{-1}_{mn})=f[d^\mathrm{i}_{kl}(\dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{mn}, U^{-1}_{mn}, F_{mn}, F^{-1}_{mn})]$.

The variables are stored as $ w_\alpha = \begin{pmatrix} d^\mathrm{i}_{11} \\ d^\mathrm{i}_{22} \\ d^\mathrm{i}_{33} \\ 2 d^\mathrm{i}_{12} \\ 2 d^\mathrm{i}_{23} \\ 2 d^\mathrm{i}_{31} \end{pmatrix} $ and $ q_\zeta = \begin{pmatrix} \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{11} \\ \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{22} \\ \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{33} \\ 2 \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{12} \\ 2 \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{23} \\ 2 \dot{U}^\mathrm{i}_{31} \\ {{U}^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{11} \\ {{U}^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{22} \\ {{U}^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{33} \\ 2 {{U}^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{12} \\ 2 {{U}^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{23} \\ 2 {{U}^\mathrm{i}}^{-1}_{31} \\ F_{11} \\ F_{12} \\ F_{13} \\ F_{21} \\ F_{22} \\ F_{23} \\ F_{31} \\ F_{32} \\ F_{33} \\ F^{-1}_{11} \\ F^{-1}_{12} \\ F^{-1}_{13} \\ F^{-1}_{21} \\ F^{-1}_{22} \\ F^{-1}_{23} \\ F^{-1}_{31} \\ F^{-1}_{32} \\ F^{-1}_{33} \end{pmatrix} $.

See also the general documentation of \textbf{ Variable\+Transform\+Function$<$double, Eigen\+::\+Vector\+Xd, Eigen\+::\+Matrix\+Xd$>$} for further information.
